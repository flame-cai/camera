XR · SLAM
H1 · XR local-floor viewer.y
WebXR requests a local-floor reference space where Y=0 is the detected floor level. The viewer pose Y coordinate is then the direct height of the camera above the floor. Uses ARCore (Android) or ARKit (iOS) SLAM — no assumptions, no calibration needed.
height = viewerPose.transform.position.y
accuracy: ±2 cm · updates: 60 fps
requires: immersive-ar session + floor surface visible

XR · Hit-Test
H2 · XR hit-test downward
Casts a ray straight down [0, −1, 0] from the viewer position. WebXR finds the nearest detected surface below (the floor) and returns its 3D position. Height = viewer.y − hit.y. Independent from H1 — uses hit-test not the reference space calibration.
height = viewerPose.y − hitResult.transform.position.y
accuracy: ±3 cm · requires: visible floor with features

XR · Planes
H3 · XR plane detection
WebXR continuously detects flat surfaces (planes) in the environment. We pick the lowest horizontal plane — the floor — and measure viewer.y minus its Y coordinate. More stable than hit-test because planes are tracked over time and filtered by ARCore/ARKit.
height = viewerPose.y − lowestHorizontalPlane.y
accuracy: ±2 cm · requires: plane-detection feature

XR · Depth
H4 · XR hardware depth buffer
On devices with hardware depth (ToF sensor or structured light), WebXR exposes a per-pixel depth map via the depth-sensing feature. We sample the bottom 15% rows of the frame (the floor region when camera tilts forward) and average them, then apply a cos(30°) correction for the viewing angle.
height = avg(depth[y > 0.85]) × cos(30°)
accuracy: ±1 cm · requires: depth-sensing feature (Android flagship)

Sensor · Baro
H5 · Barometric pressure Δ altitude
The AbsolutePressureSensor API exposes raw atmospheric pressure. Using the International Standard Atmosphere hypsometric formula, we convert pressure difference from launch to altitude change in metres. Accurate to ±0.5 m for relative changes. Available on Android Chrome (not iOS Safari).
Δh = 44330 × (1 − (P / P₀)^0.1903)
accuracy: ±0.5 m relative · auto when available

ML · Depth Anything V2
H6 · ML depth — floor region
Depth Anything V2-Small runs entirely in the browser (WebGPU or WASM) with no backend. It outputs a relative disparity map — higher values = closer. We average the bottom 15% rows (floor region) to get floor disparity, then multiply by the calibrated scale factor S to get metric height. S is auto-calibrated whenever an XR or barometric anchor is available.
height = S / floorAvgDisparity
S = metricAnchor × anchorDisparity (rolling median, n=30)
accuracy: ~5–15% · works WITHOUT XR

Derived · Trig
H7 · Trigonometric h = D · sin(|dep|)
When we know the slant distance D to the floor (from any D method) and the camera's depression angle (from gravity sensor), we can compute height geometrically. This acts as a cross-check against the sensor-based methods. Depression angle is derived from the raw gravity vector — no calibration needed.
h = D_fused × |sin(depression_rad)|
depression = asin(−gz / |g|) from gravity vector
accuracy: depends on D accuracy · fusion weight: 1.5

XR · Hit-Test
D1 · XR hit-test forward
Casts a ray from the viewer in the camera's forward direction [0, 0, −1]. The hit point is where the ray intersects the nearest detected surface. We compute 3D Euclidean distance from viewer to hit point. This is the highest-accuracy distance method — directly uses ARCore/ARKit spatial understanding.
D = √((vx−hx)²+(vy−hy)²+(vz−hz)²)
accuracy: ±1 cm · requires: visible surface ahead

XR · Depth Grid
D3 · XR depth 5×5 grid minimum
Samples a 5×5 grid of depth values in the central 40% of the frame and takes the minimum (nearest surface). More robust than single center pixel — handles cases where the nearest object is slightly off-center. The minimum gives the closest surface the camera is looking at.
D = min(depth[x∈0.3..0.7, y∈0.3..0.7])
accuracy: ±1 cm · requires: depth-sensing hardware

XR · Planes
D4 · XR plane ray intersection
Intersects the camera's forward ray with the detected floor plane. Gives the floor distance — how far the spot on the floor that the camera is pointing at is from the camera. Combined with height (H1–H3), this is geometrically exact.
D_floor = √(h² + (h/tan|dep|)²) = h / sin|dep|
accuracy: ±2 cm · requires: plane-detection + floor visible
